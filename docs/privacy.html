Privacy Policy

Short version: this website collects almost nothing, and the Urfael software is local-first. Urfael runs on your own machine, your conversations and your vault stay on your device, and we never receive your prompts, your memory, or your AI traffic. The only personal data this site can collect is an email address, and only if you choose to give us one.

The software does not talk to us. When you run Urfael, it processes your data locally on your device. Your prompts and your vault are never sent to MYG Media or to any Urfael server, because there is no Urfael server and no Urfael account. The model you point Urfael at (for example your Claude subscription, another provider, or a local model on your own hardware) has its own terms and its own privacy notice, and that connection is between you and that provider. This policy covers the marketing website at urfael.com, not the AI model you choose to use with the software.

2. What this site collects

We have deliberately built urfael.com to gather as little as possible. In normal use, the site collects two things, and only one of them depends on you:

  • A newsletter or launch email address, but only if you type it into a form and submit it. See section 3.
  • Standard server and hosting logs, created automatically by the companies that host the site, the same way almost every website on the internet works. See section 4.

We do not ask you to create an account. We do not run advertising trackers. We do not build a profile of you. We do not sell or rent personal data to anyone, ever.

4. Server logs (hosting and security)

Like virtually every website, urfael.com is served by hosting providers that automatically keep technical logs so the site can run, stay available, and be defended against attacks and abuse. These logs can include your IP address, the date and time of the request, the page or file requested, the referring page, and your browser and device type (the user-agent string).

Legal basis: legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR). The legitimate interest is operating the site reliably, keeping it secure, diagnosing faults, and preventing abuse. We do not use these logs to identify or profile individual visitors for marketing. You have the right to object to this processing (see section 10).

5. Cookies and tracking

At present, urfael.com does not set any analytics or tracking cookies, and it runs no third-party advertising or analytics trackers. There is no consent banner because there is nothing here that legally requires one. Any cookie strictly necessary to serve the site or to operate the form would be used only to make the page function, not to track you.

If we ever add analytics or any non-essential cookie in the future, we will first show a clear cookie banner and ask for your consent before any such cookie is set, and we will update this policy to describe it.

6. The Urfael software (local-first, on your machine)

Urfael is open-source software released under the MIT license that you download and run on your own computer. This is the heart of why Urfael exists, so we want to be plain about it:

  • The software processes your data locally on your device.
  • Your vault, your notes, and your memory are stored on your machine only. They are not uploaded to us.
  • MYG Media does not receive your prompts, your conversations, or your AI traffic. There is no Urfael account and no Urfael server in the loop.

When Urfael needs an AI model to think, it connects to whatever model provider you configure, for example your existing Claude subscription, another provider, or a local model running on your own hardware. That connection is directly between you and that provider, under that provider's own terms and privacy notice. We are not a party to it and we do not see it. For data handled entirely on your own machine by software you control, MYG Media is not the controller.

10. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights over your personal data. They are free to exercise:

  • Access: ask whether we hold data about you and get a copy of it.
  • Rectification: have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected.
  • Erasure: ask us to delete your data (the "right to be forgotten").
  • Restriction: ask us to limit how we use your data in certain cases.
  • Portability: receive the data you gave us in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and have it sent to another controller where technically feasible.
  • Objection: object to processing based on legitimate interest, including the server logs described in section 4.
  • Withdraw consent: where we rely on your consent (the newsletter), withdraw it at any time. This is as simple as clicking unsubscribe or emailing us, and it does not affect processing carried out before you withdrew.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@myg-media.com to confirm. We will respond within one month, as required by law. We may need to confirm your identity before acting, to protect your data.
